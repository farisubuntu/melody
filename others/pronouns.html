Urdu pronouns are words in Urdu that function as pronouns. They are not differentiated by gender, not even at the third person singular form (the same word is used for he and she,) [1] but they are more highly inflected than other parts of speech. Urdu pronouns are divided into groups including الضمائر الشخصية, ضمائر الملكية, ضمائر التأكيد, الضمائر الإنعكاسية, ضمائر الإستفهام, indefinite (الغير محدودة)pronouns, Repeated pronouns (الضمائر المكررة ), and one Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول).

الضمائر الشخصية edit

Urdu الضمائر الشخصية exist in both nominative (الإسمية) and oblique cases. The former is used when it is the subject, and the latter when followed by a postposition. Third-person pronouns have a proximal/distal distinction. All forms distinguish between singular and plural; in second-person pronouns, the plural forms can be used as both a grammatical plural and for a single person as a polite plural.

Honorifics (التفخيم)

The three nominative (الإسمية) second-person الضمائر الشخصية in Urdu all have markedly different uses in conversation.

  • تو tū is used only when addressing very young children in one's own family, one's beloved, God, or as an insult. It is commonplace in Urdu poetry, where, according to convention, it is used for one's beloved (either a lover or God).
  • تم tum is used when addressing one or more children, persons of lower status, or close, younger family members. تم سب tum sab or تم لوگ tum log can be used to emphasize plurality, akin to you all or you guys in English.
  • آپ āp is used when addressing one or more persons in most situations, including when speaking to anyone older, of higher status, deserving of respect, skilled or educated, and in all business or formal settings. It is also used by parents to children in order to teach them manners. As with tum, آپ سب āp sab or آپ لوگ āp log can be used to emphasize plurality.

As with the second-person plural pronouns, the first-person plural pronoun ہم ham can also be used in place of the singular میں ma͠i in some settings. This usage can serve to anonymize the speaker, reflect the speaker's assumption of superior status, and is widespread in poetry.

ضمائر الملكية

With other Urdu nouns, possession is shown by using the oblique case of the noun if it exists along with the genitive marker kā/kī/ke. This is not the case for first- and second-person الضمائر الشخصية, with the exception of آپ āp. Instead, unique words are used for each form, and agree with the gender/number of the object they qualify.

ضمائر التأكيد

ضمائر التأكيد in Urdu are identical to third-person الضمائر الشخصية.

3rd person/demonstrative
Proximal Distal
Singular Plural Singular Plural
nominative (الإسمية) ye یہ vo وہ
Oblique is اِس in اِن us اُس un اُن
English equivalents he, she, it, this they, these he, she, it, that they, those

These pronouns have a wide range of uses. They are narrowed down by the rest of the sentence they are used in. For example, in the sentence وہ چل رہی تھی vo cal rahī thī - "she was walking" both the progressive participle and the copula are in third-person singular female form, allowing the audience to infer voh is referring to a single female. Alternatively, in the sentence وہ ابھی خریدے ہیں voh abhī ḵẖarīde ha͠i - "those are newly-bought" both the verb and copula are in third-person form, meaning voh refers to multiple objects.

الضمائر الإنعكاسية

Urdu has a few الضمائر الإنعكاسية that are paired with proper nouns, الضمائر الشخصية, or ضمائر الملكية.

The first of these is the possessive adjective اپنا apnā - "one's own" which can also take the forms اپنى apnī and اپنے apne as it agrees with the noun it qualifies. It can be attached to a proper or personal noun in a reflexive capacity.

  • مریم اپنا کام کر رہی ہے Maryam apnā kām kar rahī hai - "Maryam is doing her own work"
  • علی اپنی گاڑی کی صفائی کر رہا ہے Alī apnī gāṛī kī safāī kar rahā hai - "Ali is cleaning his own car"
  • انہوں نے اپنے جوتے اتارے unhõ ne apne jūte utāre - "They removed their own shoes"

It can also be attached to a possessive known as an intensifier:

  • یہ میرا اپنا گھر ہے ye merā apnā ghar he - "this is my own house"

When the following can suffice without using an intensifier:

  • یہ میرا گھر ہے ye merā ghar he - "this is my house"

It is also used in the phrase اپنے آپ apne āp which is used to express an action done by oneself without help from others:

  • میں نے اپنے آپ یہ کھانا پکایا ma͠i ne apne āp ye khānā pakāyā - "I cooked this food all by myself"

Another reflexive pronoun is خود ḵẖud meaning "self" as in himself, herself, etc. It can be attached to any proper noun or personal pronoun.

  • عاصف خود سے گر گیا Āsif ḵẖud se gir gayā - "Asif fell by himself" (note usage of the instrumental marker se)
  • میں خود کتاب لکھوں گى ma͠i ḵẖud kitāb likhõ gī - "I will write a book myself"
  • آپ خود اپنا خیال رکھبں āp ḵẖud apnā ḵẖiyāl rakhẽ - "take care of yourself"

It is also used in the phrase خود بخود ḵẖud baḵẖud - "of one's own accord" when describing a sudden, generally unexpected change.

  • گھوڑا خود بخود دوڑنے لگا ghoṛā ḵẖud baḵẖud doṛne lagā - "the horse suddenly started running"

Finally, آپس میں āpas ma͠i is used to express people doing things amongst themselves reciprocally.

  • لوگ آپس میں بات کر رہے تھے log āpas ma͠i bāt kar rahe the - "the people were talking amongst themselves"

ضمائر الإستفهام

Urdu has two nominative (الإسمية) ضمائر الإستفهام, diffentiated by animacy. [2]

kyā

The inanimate interrogative is كيا kya - "what?" It can be used for singular and plural objects, while the oblique forms differentiates using كس kis for the former and كن kin for the latter.

  • یہ کیا ہے ye kyā he - "what is this?"
  • كس پر ہے kis par he - "what is it on?"
  • كن اسکولوں میں kin iskūlõ ma͠i - "in what schools?"

It can also be used to mark questions, or, rhetorically, an exclamation.

  • كيا یہ تمہارا ہے kyā ye tumhārā he - "is this yours?"
  • كيا خوبصورت چہرہ ہے kyā ḵẖubsūrat cehra he - "what a beautiful face!"

kaun

The animate equivalent of kyā is كون kaun - "who?" It too can be used for either singular or plural objects, and its oblique forms are identical to their inanimate equivalents.

  • یہ كون ہے ye kaun he - "who is this?"
  • کس کو دوں kis ko dũ - "to whom do I give (it)?"
  • كن سے لوں kin se lũ - "from whom all do I take (it)?"

It is paired with the semblative marker sā/sī/se to ask "which?" of a finite number of possibilities.

  • کون سا لڑکا kaun sā laṛkā - "which boy?"

Indefinite pronouns

Urdu has two indefinite (الغير محدودة)pronouns, کوئی koī - "somebody" and کچھ kuch - "something". The latter is used also as an adjective and an adverb, meaning "some", "a few", "partly," and the latter can also be used as an adverb meaning "some", "about".

Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول)

Urdu has one commonly used Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول), جو jo - "who", "which".

  • ہم جو خوش ہیں ham jo ḵẖusẖ ha͠i - "we who are happy"
  • وه جو اچھا ہے vo jo accha he - "that which is good"

In nearly all daily usage, جو jo is used as both an animate and inanimate Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول). However, in some poetry, a rarer Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول) سو so is used as an inanimate Relative pronoun (الإسم الموصول). This feature is also preserved in the dialect of Urdu spoken in Bangalore

Repeated pronouns (الضمائر المكررة )

كثير من الضمائر أعلاه يمكن لها ان تتكرر لتنشئ معاني جديدة

The inanimate(الجامدة) interrogative pronoun is reduplicated to form كيا كيا kyā kyā - "which (various) things?" as in the question وہاں کیا كيا کھایا vahã kyā kyā khāyā - "what all did you eat there?"

Similarly, the animate interrogative pronoun - (ضمير الاستفهام المتحرك) reduplicated forms كون كون kaun kaun - "which (various) people?" which takes a singular verb as in یہ كون كون کھایا ye kaun kaun khāyā - "who all ate this?"

There are two ways to reduplicate the animate indefinite (الغير محدودة)pronoun with two different meanings: كوئى كوئى koī koī - "a few" as in صرف كوئى كوئى بچا sirf koī koī bacā - "only a few remained", or كوئى نہ كوئى koī na koī - "someone or another" as in كوئى نہ كوئى ہو گا koī na koī ho gā - "someone or another will be (there)." The oblique form can also be reduplicated in the same manner: کسی نہ کسی کو پتا ہو گا kisī na kisī ko patā ho gā - "someone or another will know."

In the same way, the inanimate indefinite (الغير محدودة)pronoun can also be reduplicated in two ways:

  • موسم کچھ کچھ اچھا ہوا mausam kuch kuch acchā huā - "the weather got somewhat better"
  • کچھ نہ کچھ ہوتے رہتا ہے kuch na kuch hote rehta he - "something or another keeps happening"
